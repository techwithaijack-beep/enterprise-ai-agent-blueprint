## LinkedIn launch post
Most enterprise AI teams can describe their architecture.

Far fewer can **assess whether it is actually production-ready**.

That’s the gap I wanted to make easier to close.

I’ve added a new reusable asset to my Enterprise AI Agent Blueprint:

**Enterprise AI Control Plane Assessment Scorecard**

It helps teams evaluate whether an AI assistant, RAG solution, or agent workflow is still operating like a pilot — or has the controls needed for real enterprise use.

It covers 10 dimensions:
- business ownership
- knowledge layer quality
- retrieval trust
- workflow vs agent boundaries
- policy and access controls
- tool integration discipline
- human approval handling
- observability and auditability
- evaluation/release governance
- business outcome instrumentation

The reason I think this matters:
A lot of AI projects stall not because the model is weak, but because the operating model around knowledge, tools, controls, and measurement is underbuilt.

If a team cannot score those areas honestly, it usually is not dealing with a model problem.
It is dealing with an architecture and governance problem.

This scorecard is designed to be practical:
- usable in workshops
- useful in proposal discovery
- relevant for internal architecture reviews
- simple enough to drive a 30/60/90-day plan

If you’re building enterprise RAG, copilots, or agent systems, this is the question I’d ask:

**Which control-plane dimension is most underinvested in your environment today?**

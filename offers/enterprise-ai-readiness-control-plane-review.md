# Enterprise AI Readiness & Control Plane Review

A practical advisory offer for enterprise teams that have an AI assistant, RAG solution, or agent workflow in motion and need to determine whether it is genuinely ready for production.

This offer is designed to turn architecture discussions into a concrete operating roadmap.

## Best fit

This review is a strong fit when a team has one or more of the following:
- a pilot that works in demos but feels risky in production
- a RAG or agent solution with unclear governance boundaries
- fragmented knowledge sources and inconsistent retrieval quality
- no clear workflow vs agent decision model
- weak traceability, approval handling, or outcome measurement
- pressure to move from POC to enterprise rollout quickly

## Target buyers

- CIO / CTO office
- enterprise AI platform owners
- customer service transformation leaders
- knowledge management and operations leaders
- delivery partners who need a stronger production architecture story

## Core promise

In 2 weeks, identify whether the current AI solution is operating like a governed production system or a promising pilot, then define the highest-value control-plane improvements needed for the next 30/60/90 days.

## Review questions

This engagement is designed to answer questions like:
- Is the current architecture production-capable or pilot-only?
- Is retrieval grounded, governed, and measurable enough?
- Where should we use workflows vs agentic behavior?
- Which actions need approval, and how should that work operationally?
- Are policy, observability, evaluation, and release controls sufficient?
- What is the most practical roadmap to reduce risk without slowing delivery to a halt?

## Scope

The review is centered around the following dimensions:
1. business ownership and scope
2. knowledge layer quality
3. retrieval trust and grounding
4. workflow vs agent boundaries
5. policy and access controls
6. tool integration discipline
7. human approval and exception handling
8. observability and auditability
9. evaluation and release governance
10. business outcome instrumentation

## Delivery format

### Option A — 2-week advisory sprint
Recommended default.

#### Week 1
- stakeholder intake and use-case framing
- architecture and operating-model review
- scorecard-based assessment workshop
- evidence capture across architecture, controls, and operations

#### Week 2
- gap synthesis and risk prioritization
- target-state control-plane recommendations
- 30/60/90-day roadmap
- playback session with leadership and delivery owners

### Option B — single workshop + readout
Best for lightweight discovery.

Includes:
- 60-90 minute assessment workshop
- high-level gap summary
- top 3 priorities
- recommended next-step sprint

## Deliverables

### 1. Executive summary
A concise summary of:
- what is working
- where production risk sits
- what needs to change first
- why it matters to business outcomes

### 2. Scorecard output
Completed assessment across the 10 control-plane dimensions, with:
- scores
- evidence notes
- major gaps
- interpretation of current maturity

### 3. Current-state risk map
A plain-language view of the biggest delivery and governance risks, such as:
- knowledge quality/freshness gaps
- insufficient approval controls
- tool access risk
- missing observability
- weak release governance
- KPI blind spots

### 4. Target-state recommendations
A practical architecture and operating-model recommendation set covering:
- control-plane improvements
- workflow vs agent routing guidance
- approval design
- observability and evaluation priorities
- policy and environment boundary improvements

### 5. 30/60/90-day roadmap
A sequenced action plan with near-term priorities that can realistically be executed by delivery teams.

## Suggested agenda

### Kickoff / discovery
- business objective and target use case
- current architecture and delivery status
- current blockers or executive concerns
- desired timeline and rollout pressure

### Assessment workshop
- score the 10 dimensions collaboratively
- challenge assumptions with evidence
- identify the 3 most material risks
- agree the target maturity level required

### Playback session
- summarize findings
- align on priorities
- confirm owners and sequencing
- define whether the next step is implementation support, governance design, or architecture refinement

## Example findings this offer can surface

- "The pilot works, but approval handling is informal and not auditable."
- "Retrieval quality is acceptable, but source ownership and freshness controls are weak."
- "Agent autonomy is being used where deterministic workflows would be safer and easier to operate."
- "The team measures answer quality but cannot show workflow impact or business value."
- "Tool integrations exist, but timeout/error/idempotency behavior is inconsistent."
